### Best Matches (H5AD + Human + ≥10 samples)

**Strict matches** (human islets + H5AD + ≥10 samples):

- **[GSE203384](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE203384)** is the cleanest hit: 10 human iPSC-derived β-cell samples with a ready-to-use `processed_data.h5ad.gz` (1.7 GB). Note these are iPSC-derived, not primary donor islets.
- **[GSE198623](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE198623)** has excellent H5AD files for human islets (`human_processed.h5ad.gz`), but only 7 total samples — it falls just short of the ≥10 threshold. However, the human-specific H5AD is well-annotated and widely used for cross-species comparisons.

### Strong Alternatives Without H5AD (but large, well-characterized human islet datasets):

- **[GSE148073](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE148073)** — 14 samples, primary human islets with T1D context; no H5AD but large RAW.tar
- **[GSE86469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE86469)** — 638 GSMs (individual cells as samples), classic T2D landmark dataset; processed CSV available
- **[GSE214517](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE214517)** — 2,785 Smart-seq2 cells, large reference; no H5AD
- **[GSE274264](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE274264)** — 10 samples, CVB3 infection model in primary human islets; data in .rds format only

### Key Caveats

> ⚠️ **H5AD scarcity**: Very few human pancreatic islet scRNA-seq datasets in GEO provide `.h5ad` files. Most use `.rds` (R/Seurat), `.mtx.gz`, or plain text matrices. If H5AD is essential, **GSE203384** and **GSE198623** are your primary options, though both have limitations (iPSC-derived or slightly under 10 human samples, respectively).

> ⚠️ **n_samples vs. n_cells**: Datasets like GSE81608 (1,600) and GSE214517 (2,785) use individual cells as GEO Samples (Smart-seq2 style), not biological donor replicates. GSE148073 (14 samples = 14 donors/conditions) is more representative of true biological replication.

> ⚠️ **Stem cell-derived vs. primary islets**: GSE203384, GSE199636, and GSE167880 profile SC-derived or iPSC-derived islets, not primary cadaveric donor tissue. If primary human islets are required, prioritize GSE148073, GSE86469, GSE274264, or GSE196715.

## 🔑 Highlights & Caveats

**Direct H5AD hits (≥10 samples):**
- **[GSE280267](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE280267)** is the **strongest match** — 199 samples with a directly downloadable `.h5ad` file alongside R data objects, focusing on T2D β cell dysfunction.

**Large-scale datasets (no native H5AD, but highly usable processed data):**
- **[GSE86469](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE86469)** (638 samples) and **[GSE251913](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE251913)** (92 samples) are among the largest islet scRNA-seq studies and provide `.csv.gz` matrices easily convertible to H5AD via AnnData.
- **[GSE221156](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE221156)** (54 samples) is a well-annotated T2D atlas.

**Caveats:**
- Most RAW `.tar` archives contain 10x Genomics mtx/barcode/feature trios, readily importable into AnnData/Seurat but not pre-packaged as `.h5ad`.
- **[GSE266291](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE266291)** is snRNA-seq (nuclei, not cells) — fine for most gene expression analyses but worth noting.
- **[GSE198623](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE198623)** has native `.h5ad` files but only 7 human GSM samples (below threshold).
