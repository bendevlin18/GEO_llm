### 🔍 Best Matches & Notes

- **[GSE276860](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE276860) & [GSE276861](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE276861)** are the most H3K27me3-focused datasets — both target H3K27me3 + SUZ12 (a PRC2 subunit) in **K562 cells** with CRAMP1/SUZ12 depletion. They are a matched CUT&RUN/CUT&Tag pair, ideal for cross-method comparison.

- **[GSE253630](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE253630)** is notable for profiling H3K27me3 across **three distinct human kidney anatomical regions** (cortex, medulla, papilla) with clean IgG controls and processed BigWig files — great for tissue-level epigenomics.

- **[GSE253289](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE253289)** (38 samples) is the largest dataset here, studying **PRC1 (BMI1/RING1B)** activity alongside H3K27me3 in human erythroid cells — useful for Polycomb biology studies.

- **[GSE164594](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE164594)** and **[GSE241443](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE241443)** both investigate **X-chromosome inactivation (XIST/XCI)** via H3K27me3, with allele-resolved (maternal/paternal) data in B cells and naïve pluripotent stem cells, respectively.

### ⚠️ Caveats
- **GSE160468** includes H3K27me3 peaks as part of a broader SMARCA5/CTCF study — H3K27me3 is not the primary target but is included in the processed files.
- **GSE241443** uses **CUT&Tag** (not CUT&RUN), which has slightly different technical characteristics.
- All datasets have processed data available (BigWig, BED, or RAW.tar files).
