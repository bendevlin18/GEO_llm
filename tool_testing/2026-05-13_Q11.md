### Best Matches & Notes

**Top-tier brain-specific datasets:**

- **GSE246443** (n=42) and **GSE149871** (n=70) are the largest purely human brain ATAC-seq datasets here, covering **dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (BA9)** postmortem tissue in psychiatric disease contexts (MDD/suicide). Both have processed count matrices available — ideal for differential accessibility analysis.
- **GSE264549** (n=69) is notable for **Parkinson's disease** with paired hemisphere samples from prefrontal cortex — one of the largest PD chromatin accessibility resources in GEO.
- **GSE83345** profiles **postmortem brain** with a focus on schizophrenia non-coding loci; the large RAW.tar (12.8GB) suggests raw reads are the primary deliverable.
- **GSE96614** covers **four distinct brain regions** (NAcc, BA9, BA24, hippocampus) with flow-sorted nuclei — excellent for region-specific regulatory analysis. The pre-processed counts .txt.gz is directly usable. ✅ Processed data available
- **GSE149268** is a **developmental atlas** of the human telencephalon across nine anatomical regions — best choice for developmental or regulatory element discovery work. Both hg19 and hg38 annotation BEDs are provided. ✅ Processed data available
- **GSE214911** focuses on **Alzheimer's disease** snATAC-seq (105,332 nuclei) with snRNA-seq integration; note the title says ChIP-seq but the modality is ATAC-seq — verify on GEO.
- **GSE166016** is specifically designed to **benchmark tissue preprocessing methods** for snATAC-seq — valuable for methods-focused or QC-heavy projects.
- **GSE226690** covers **human microglia maturation** — relevant if glial/immune cell chromatin accessibility in brain is the focus.

**Important caveats:**
- ⚠️ **GSE245957** is a multi-species, multi-tissue dataset; human cortex data is included but not the exclusive focus.
- ⚠️ Several datasets (GSE83345, GSE129040, GSE166016) provide primarily raw data in RAW.tar archives — downstream peak calling will be required.
- ⚠️ **GSE115882/GSE115881/GSE115883** appeared in the results with "neuroscience" tags but are actually **kidney DNase-seq** datasets — excluded from the table above as they are not brain tissue.
- ⚠️ **GSE95023** covers developing (fetal) neocortex, not adult brain — relevant for neurodevelopment but not adult/disease contexts.

## 🔬 Highlights & Recommendations

### 🏆 Top Atlas/Reference Datasets
- **[GSE211822](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE211822)** (n=210) — The broadest human brain ATAC-seq resource, covering **25 brain regions** across neurons and glia. Ideal for region-specific regulatory element analysis.
- **[GSE96949](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE96949)** (n=115) — A comprehensive adult human brain chromatin atlas, widely cited for neuropsychiatric GWAS annotation.
- **[GSE244618](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE244618)** (n=128) — Single-cell chromatin accessibility atlas of the human brain (processed data available ✅).
- **[GSE147672](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE147672)** (n=162) — scATAC-seq with processed `.rds.gz` files ✅; focused on Alzheimer's and Parkinson's disease risk loci.

### 🧠 Disease-Focused
- **Neuropsychiatric/Schizophrenia**: GSE83345, GSE96614, GSE143666
- **Alzheimer's & Parkinson's**: GSE147672, GSE295832
- **Rett Syndrome**: GSE117507
- **Glioma/DIPG (brain tumors)**: GSE268109, GSE275224, GSE276283
- **HIV in brain**: GSE221686

### 🌱 Developmental Datasets
- GSE132403, GSE149268, GSE131256 (hippocampus), GSE215891 (organoids), GSE252523, GSE196074

### ⚠️ Caveats
- Some datasets include **non-human comparisons** (e.g., GSE192772 includes chimp/macaque; GSE154068 includes mouse).
- Several "brain" datasets use **organoids or iPSC-derived neurons**, not primary tissue (GSE215891, GSE132403, GSE252523) — verify experimental design before use.
- Processed data files (`.rds`, `.h5`, `.mtx.gz`, `.csv.gz`) are available for GSE147672, GSE204682, GSE192772, GSE252523, and GSE244618.
