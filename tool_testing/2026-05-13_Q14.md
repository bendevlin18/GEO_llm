### Best Matches & Notes

**Top picks by use case:**

- **Human cardiac disease (spatial):** [GSE135805](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE135805) is the most direct match — human heart sections across different failure etiologies profiled by spatial transcriptomics. [GSE114770](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE114770) adds arrhythmogenic cardiomyopathy context in human tissue. [GSE298023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE298023) is the most recent (2024) and largest human dataset, focusing on fibroblast heterogeneity in failing hearts with processed .rds files included.

- **Myocardial infarction / border zone biology:** [GSE176092](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE176092) is the largest dataset (1,118 samples) combining spatial + snRNA-seq for post-MI remodeling in mouse. [GSE269054](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE269054) spans both mouse and human MI, focusing on IFN responses at injury borders, with processed data available (.tar.gz, 1.5GB).

- **Heart failure mechanisms:** [GSE168742](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE168742) is a major multimodal resource (1,413 samples, mouse + human, spatial + scRNA-seq) examining fibroblast-driven heart failure via the Htra3–TGF-β axis, with abundant processed .csv.gz files. [GSE218759](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE218759) focuses on HFpEF and cardiac reprogramming using 10x Visium.

- **Neonatal regeneration:** [GSE188888](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE188888) (very large raw files at 51.8GB) and [GSE254055](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE254055) both address neonatal heart regeneration spatially.

- **COVID-19 cardiac impact:** [GSE212119](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE212119) uses targeted spatial transcriptomics (not 10x Visium) on autopsy cardiac tissue from SARS-CoV-2 patients — note the small file sizes suggest a targeted panel approach rather than whole-transcriptome Visium.

### Highlights & Notes

- 🏆 **Best for human cardiac disease studies**: [GSE298023](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE298023) (failing heart fibroblasts, 18 samples, processed .rds), [GSE290367](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE290367) (multiomic atlas, 90 samples, processed .h5ad), and [GSE317680](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE317680) (allograft vasculopathy, single-cell + spatial).
- 🏆 **Best for myocardial infarction/injury**: [GSE176092](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE176092) is the largest MI dataset (1118 samples), and [GSE168742](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE168742) is a large cross-species (mouse + human) heart failure dataset with extensive processed .csv.gz files.
- 🏆 **Best for heart development**: [GSE244723](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE244723) (human embryonic heart) and [GSE283967](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/geo/query/acc.cgi?acc=GSE283967) (first-trimester human heart atlas) are particularly valuable.
- ⚠️ **SuperSeries caveat**: GSE188892 is a SuperSeries — the actual spatial data is in the SubSeries **GSE188888** (spatial Visium) and GSE188844 (RNA-seq).
- 📦 **Processed data available**: GSE168742 (.csv.gz), GSE244723 (.csv.gz), GSE298023 (.rds), GSE290367 (.h5ad.gz), and GSE176092 (.xlsx) all have readily usable processed files.
- 🐟 **Non-mammalian models**: GSE149457 (chicken), GSE241156 (zebrafish + mouse TOMOseq), and GSE324074 (rat Stereo-seq) offer unique comparative/regeneration perspectives.
